Best cat breeds for beginner pet owners

A list of best beginner cat breeds and absolutely kawaii kitties

Ragdoll cats

Ragdolls are known for their relaxed nature and love being around people. They often go limp when picked up, hence the name "ragdoll." They are generally easy to train and get along well with children and other pets

British shorthair

British shorthairs are generally low energy and don't require as much attention as other breeds. They are happy to be left alone for periods and don't demand constant interaction, making them a good choice for busy individuals or families.

American shorthair

Low maintenance, with short fur, but their social needs are high. They don’t like being left alone for long periods.Talkative, affectionate, and intelligent. They love being the center of attention and will form strong bonds with their owners.

Manx

Grooming: Their short or medium coat is easy to maintain. Regular brushing is recommended for the longer-haired variety. Health: Known for being generally healthy, though some Manx can have specific genetic conditions related to their tail. Temperament: Friendly, intelligent, and easygoing. They don’t demand excessive attention or grooming

Siamese

Grooming: Short coat, so they require very little grooming. A weekly brushing is typically enoughHealth: Generally healthy but can be prone to dental issues, so regular checkups are important.Temperament: Social, affectionate, and very interactive but still fairly easy to care for.
